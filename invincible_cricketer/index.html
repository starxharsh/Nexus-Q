Why He Would Be Untouchable Today

Sir Don Bradman played in an era of uncovered pitches, no helmets, and heavy bats made of raw timber. Despite these "disadvantages," he maintained an average that is nearly 40 runs higher than the next best player.

If he played now:

  • Modern Gear: With high-carbon protective gear and aerodynamically optimized bats, his scoring rate would skyrocket.
  • Video Analysis: Bradman was a master of technique. Today’s data-driven insights would have allowed him to dismantle every bowling plan before the first ball.
  • Fitness Standards: His hand-eye coordination was legendary. Modern sports science would have turned him into an athletic powerhouse.
